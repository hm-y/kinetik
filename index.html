ADVANCED BIOMEDICAL
MATERIALS AND DEVICES

We bring advanced materials based solutions from the research laboratories to real life surgical applications. We are initially focused on indications in neurosurgical and spinal surgeries using fiber-based artificial muscles.

We are prepared for other indications such as non-invasive monitoring (arterial line, intracranial pressure monitoring), engraftment to nerves, the charging of implantable batteries via energy harvesting systems, and metabolic monitoring via wearable technologies. All of these technologies are centered on the smart fibers which are at the core of our business.

Artificial “muscles” achieve powerful pulling force
3 days ago
MIT News

As a cucumber plant grows, it sprouts tightly coiled tendrils that seek out supports in order to pull the plant upward. This ensures the plant receives as much sunlight exposure as possible. Now, researchers at MIT have found a way to imitate this coiling-and-pulling mechanism to produce contracting fibers that could be used as artificial muscles for robots, prosthetic limbs, or other mechanical and biomedical applications. A SYNERGETIC TEAM OF EXPERTS

Mehmet Kanik

CEO, CTO

Dr. Kanik is the inventor of fiber-based artificial muscle. His research at Massachusetts Institute of Technology has led to breakthroughs in materials science and was published and highlighted on the cover in Science. His work was also highlighted in world news more than 500 times in 20 different languages. He holds a PhD in Materials Science and Nanotechnology.

Ian Tafel

CMO

Dr. Tafel is extensively trained in spinal surgery. He is currently the Chief Resident Neurosurgeon at Harvard Medical School, Massachusetts General Hospital, and Brigham and Women’s Hospital. His research background is in spinal cord injury repair with optogenics and nerve scaffolding.


Jason Chen

BD

Dr. Chen is a scientist-entrepreneur with an extensive background in the biotechnology sector. He co-founded the San Francisco-based pharmaceutical company, Verge Genomics, and fundraised more than $30 million in the company's Series A. He was recognized as one of Forbes Magazine's 30 Under 30, and his academic work has been cited over 1,000 times. He completed his MD and PhD at the University of California, Los Angeles, and a postdoctoral fellowship at the Massachusetts General Hospital in Boston, Massachusetts.

Joshua Bernstock

COO

As an NIH Oxford-Cambridge Scholar and surgeon-scientist, Dr. Bernstock has diverse medical knowledge with unique expertise in the repositioning of approved drugs and the development/characterization of novel/experimental therapeutics. He completed his MD at the University of Alabama Birmingham and his PhD at the University of Cambridge and is a Resident Neurosurgeon at Brigham and Women’s and Boston Children’s Hospitals in Boston, Massachusetts.
